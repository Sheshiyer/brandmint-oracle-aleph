[BRAND NAME]. Act as a Visionary Industrial Designer and Master Food Stylist / Luxury Stylist.

PHASE 1: ADAPTIVE OBJECT TRANSFORMATION. 

The core subject is a Jerry Can (fuel canister), radically reimagined as a physical high-end artifact that embodies the DNA of [BRAND NAME]. The generator must autonomously transform the canister based on the brand's industry:

- If FASHION: Morph the canister into a luxury accessory or handbag. Integrate leather textures, high-end stitching, designer handles, and signature monogram patterns directly into the canister's form.

- If BEAUTY/PERFUME: Morph the canister into a giant, sophisticated perfume bottle. Use materials like frosted glass, gold filigree, and crystalline caps while maintaining the recognizable silhouette of the Jerry Can.

- If TECH/AUTOMOTIVE: Morph the canister into a sleek, futuristic device using seamless metals, carbon fiber, and glowing LED accents.

- If FOOD/BEVERAGE: Morph the canister into an inviting food or drink item.

    - For FOOD (e.g., Godiva, Ladurée): Transform into a gourmet chocolate box, a high-end cake, or an intricate dessert sculpture. Focus on edible textures like ganache, frosting, and delicate confectionery.

    - For BEVERAGE (e.g., Coca-Cola, Starbucks): Transform into a premium beverage dispenser, a stylized cocktail shaker, or a frosted glass bottle. Focus on condensation, liquid clarity, and refreshing textures.

PHASE 2: HYPER-TACTILE TEXTURE & SENSORY REALISM. 

The object must look 100% tangible and physically real. Focus on extreme macro-detail: 

- Render the grain of the leather, the cold touch of the metal, the refraction of light through glass, the melt of chocolate, or the condensation on a cold drink. 

- Include micro-imperfections (subtle scratches, dust, or organic texture) to ensure the object looks like a real-world museum piece or a tantalizing culinary creation.

PHASE 3: COMPOSITION & ENVIRONMENT. 

- BACKGROUND: Place the object in the center of a pure, clinical white studio cyclorama (infinite white space with no horizon). 

- POSITIONING: The object should be medium-sized and levitating slightly, rotated at a dynamic 3D angle (3/4 view) to reveal its depth and unique modified features.

- NO LOGOS: Do not include any external text or floating logos in the frame. The brand should be recognizable solely through the object's design language, colors, and iconic textures.

PHASE 4: LIGHTING & SHADOWS. 

High-key studio lighting with soft, wrapping lights. Elegant specular highlights to define the object's shape. A soft, realistic contact shadow must appear on the white surface below the levitating object to ground it in space. For food/beverage, consider subtle rim lighting to enhance freshness.

TECH SPECS: 

100mm Macro lens, f/11 for deep focus, Ray Tracing (Path Tracing), Subsurface Scattering (especially for food/liquid), 8k resolution, ultra-clean commercial product photography style.
